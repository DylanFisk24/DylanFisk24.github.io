This tutoring philosophy was created for the first of two courses I was required to take to become a tutor at UMass Amherst. It lays out the beliefs I hold about writing, tutoring, and the role of writing centers as well as the essential components of my practice as a tutor. Writing this has given me a new perspective on what it means to be a writer and how we can best come alongside those who are struggling with the process.

Paradise Lost Analysis Essay

I wrote this essay for an early British literature course I took at UMass Amherst. It dissects the famous English epic Paradise Lost by John Milton in order to understand the rhetorical strategies present in Satan’s arguments toward Eve in the garden of Eden. This work demonstrates my ability to deeply understand and analyze text, combining the text’s own language with my interpretation to bring about novel ideas.
